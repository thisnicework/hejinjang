About

He Jin Jang Dance is a choreographic project group rooted in embodied research, collective ritual, and artistic kinship — led by He Jin Jang.

The work investigates the body as a site of cultural memory, diasporic experience, and sensory knowledge. Drawing from Korean shamanic practices, somatic traditions, and post-colonial theory, He Jin Jang develops performance works that unfold across time, space, and community.

He Jin Jang is a Singapore-based choreographer, performer, and researcher. Her practice spans performance, installation, and pedagogy — with a sustained inquiry into the politics of the sensing body, collective care, and the ethics of appearance.

She has presented work internationally, including at the Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay (Singapore), the Seoul Arts Center, the Royal Academy of Dance (London), and at various festivals across Europe and Asia.

He Jin Jang holds a Master of Fine Arts in Choreography from the London Contemporary Dance School and has been supported by the National Arts Council Singapore, the Korea Arts Foundation, and various international residency programs.
